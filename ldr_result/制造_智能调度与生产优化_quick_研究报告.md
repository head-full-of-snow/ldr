   The convergence of Digital Twin (DT) technology with real-time scheduling has moved from static modeling to dynamic, predictive simulation. Critical reflection suggests that while DTs improve accuracy, the computational latency of high-fidelity simulations remains a bottleneck for real-time decision-making. 3. **Reinforcement Learning for Dynamic Environments**
   Reinforcement Learning, particularly Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL), has become a cornerstone for handling dynamic disruptions such as machine breakdowns or rush orders. Recent studies highlight the success of multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) in complex, decentralized production systems, where individual agents coordinate to optimize global objectives [6]. Transfer learning and domain adaptation techniques are currently being refined to address this [7].

5. **Human-AI Collaboration in Scheduling**
   A growing trend is the shift from fully automated scheduling to human-AI collaborative systems. AI provides optimal or near-optimal schedules, while human experts provide contextual insights and override AI recommendations when necessary. This approach leverages the interpretability of AI decisions through explainable AI (XAI) techniques, fostering trust and acceptance among shop floor managers [11]. Research shows that hybrid systems often outperform fully automated ones in complex, non-standard scenarios [12].

The latest research in intelligent scheduling and production optimization is characterized by the integration of AI (especially LLMs and RL), Digital Twins, and sustainability metrics.
