Recent research in 2026 highlights significant advancements in the integration of knowledge graphs (KGs) with artificial intelligence to enhance educational outcomes, particularly in specialized fields and through multimodal data processing.

In the domain of basic medical education, the application of AI intelligent tutoring systems (ITS) combined with knowledge graphs has shown promising effectiveness in teaching pathological concepts. This integration allows for a more structured and personalized learning experience by mapping complex medical relationships and tailoring instruction to individual student needs [1]. The use of knowledge graphs helps in visualizing and understanding the intricate connections between pathological mechanisms, which is crucial for medical students' comprehension and retention of knowledge.

Furthermore, the development of intelligent educational decision-making systems driven by multimodal data fusion and knowledge graphs represents another key area of progress. These systems leverage data from various sources, such as text, audio, video, and behavioral metrics, to create a comprehensive understanding of the learner's state and progress. By fusing these multimodal data streams with knowledge graphs, educators and AI systems can make more informed decisions regarding curriculum adjustments, resource allocation, and personalized interventions [2]. This approach not only enhances the accuracy of educational assessments but also facilitates a more adaptive and responsive learning environment.

Critically, while these advancements offer substantial benefits, they also raise considerations regarding data privacy, the accuracy of multimodal data interpretation, and the potential for bias in AI-driven decision-making. The effectiveness of these systems depends heavily on the quality and comprehensiveness of the underlying knowledge graphs and the robustness of the multimodal data fusion algorithms. Therefore, ongoing research and validation are necessary to ensure that these technologies are deployed ethically and effectively in educational settings.
