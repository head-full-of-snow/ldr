According to [1], researchers have introduced ToggleMimic, a two-stage policy designed for text-driven humanoid whole-body control. This approach addresses key limitations in previous imitation learning methods, which often relied on rigid task specifications or single-modal inputs. These traditional methods struggled to interpret high-level semantic instructions, such as natural language commands, or to dynamically switch between different behaviors. By utilizing natural language as an essential communication medium, ToggleMimic aims to enable humanoid robots to interact more naturally with humans and integrate seamlessly into daily life. The system allows robots to acquire complex motions through expert demonstrations while overcoming the previous inability to process high-level semantic information effectively.

This development represents a shift towards more flexible and semantically aware control systems, allowing humanoid robots to respond to intuitive human language rather than just pre-programmed or rigidly defined tasks.
